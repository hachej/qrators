# Vibe Coding and the Maker Movement

**By Sachin**  
**Published:** February 26, 2026

---

## Overview

This essay examines vibe coding through the lens of the Maker Movement (2005-2015), drawing parallels between how communities form around emerging technologies and how value ultimately flows to infrastructure layers rather than individual creators.

## Key Arguments

**The Maker Movement Parallel**

The author identifies structural similarities between vibe coding and the Maker Movement, including low-quality outputs ("crapjects" vs. "slop") and the presence of visible network intellectuals who gained influence through participation and documentation.

**Missing Scenius Phase**

Unlike previous hobbyist technology waves, vibe coding skipped the critical "scenius" phase—a protected playground where communities develop judgment through playful experimentation. Instead, it deployed directly to enterprise, creating what the author terms "evaluative anesthesia" where creators cannot distinguish between genuinely useful work and mere productivity stimulation.

**Value Capture Pattern**

Both phenomena demonstrate Joel Spolsky's "commoditizing your complement" dynamic: rapid prototyping tools democratize one layer while concentrating value upstream. With vibe coding, infrastructure providers capture value from user-generated signal rather than individual creators.

## Reframing Consumption

Rather than adopting the salvation-through-making narrative, the author proposes understanding vibe coding as **consumption of surplus cognitive energy**. This reframe enables four value-capture strategies:

- **Taste development** through iterative creation
- **Attention generation** via performance of making
- **Gift economies** through open-source contribution  
- **Signal capture** by documenting what fails and why

The consumption metaphor, the author argues, is psychologically sustainable because it abandons craft's transformational expectations.
