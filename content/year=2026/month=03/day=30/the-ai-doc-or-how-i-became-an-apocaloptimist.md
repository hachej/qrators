# The AI Doc: Or How I Became an Apocaloptimist

## Overview

"The AI Doc: Or How I Became an Apocaloptimist" is a 2026 American documentary film that explores artificial intelligence through the personal lens of filmmaker Daniel Roher as he prepares to become a father. The film investigates the existential threats and potential promises of AI, balancing anxiety with "apocaloptimism."

## Production Details

- **Directors:** Daniel Roher and Charlie Tyrell
- **Producers:** Daniel Kwan, Jonathan Wang, Shane Boris, Diane Becker, Ted Tremper
- **Cinematography:** Jenni Morello, Lowell A. Meyer
- **Editors:** Davis Coombe, Daysha Broadway
- **Music:** Marius de Vries, Matt Robertson
- **Production Companies:** Playgrounds, Cottage M, Fishbowl Films
- **Runtime:** 104 minutes (1 hour 43 minutes)
- **Language:** English
- **Rating:** PG-13

## Synopsis

Told through the perspective of filmmaker Daniel Roher as he prepares to become a father, the documentary conducts interviews with leading experts in the field to understand the world his child will inherit. The film is described as "a hand-made, eye-opening documentary about the most powerful technology humanity has ever created... and what's at stake if we get it wrong."

## Featured Experts

The documentary features interviews with leading AI experts including:
- Sam Altman (OpenAI)
- Dr. Dario Amodei (Anthropic)
- Demis Hassabis (Google DeepMind)
